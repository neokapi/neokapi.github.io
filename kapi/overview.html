Kapi

Kapi parses content in any format, lets you (or your AI agent) edit and check the content inside it, and writes the original back byte-for-byte — then makes that content work in every language. It is built on the open-source neokapi content engine.

Two jobs, one engine:

  • Keep your source content right — edit it faithfully and verify it with checks for terminology, QA, and brand voice that act like tests for AI output (a draft scores 0–100 and a gate passes or fails).
  • Make it multilingual — translate it with AI or MT, reuse what you've already translated, enforce your terminology, and hand bilingual files to translators — all without ever mangling the file's structure.

Two ways to run it​

Kapi comes in two forms that share the same engine, tools, flows, and .kapi projects — a workflow built in one runs in the other:

  • Kapi CLI — a single binary that operates directly on files. Best for automation, scripting, CI, offline work, and for serving tools to your AI assistant over MCP.
  • Kapi Desktop — a visual app for building flows, running tools, managing plugins, and storing credentials — no YAML, no flags.

Not sure which fits? See Kapi CLI or Kapi Desktop?

How you'll work​

You drive Kapi either ad-hoc (one-off, on files you name) or as a project (a .kapi recipe plus a store that remembers your translations) — the same commands, a different binding. See Projects vs ad-hoc.

From there it's task by task: the Kapi CLI recipes walk preparing your source and going multilingual, and Kapi Desktop does the same work visually.
